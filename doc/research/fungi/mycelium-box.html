Pasteurization

To grow oyster mushrooms using calcium oxide for pasteurization, follow this detailed manual. This method involves preparing the substrate, pasteurizing it with a high-pH lime bath, inoculating with mushroom spawn, and creating the right conditions for fruiting.

Step-by-Step Guide to Growing Oyster Mushrooms

1. Materials Needed

  • Oyster Mushroom Spawn: Obtain high-quality spawn from a reputable supplier.
  • Substrate: Common substrates include straw, sawdust, or coffee grounds.
  • Calcium Oxide (Lime): Used for creating a high-pH environment for pasteurization.
  • Water: For soaking and mixing.
  • Containers or Bags: For holding the substrate during incubation and fruiting.
  • Thermometer: To monitor temperature.
  • Spray Bottle: For misting during fruiting.

2. Preparing the Substrate

Substrate Selection

Choose a suitable substrate. Straw is commonly used due to its availability and nutrient content.

Preparation Steps

  1. Chop the Straw: Cut the straw into small pieces (about 1-3 inches long) to increase surface area.
  2. Soak in Water: Soak the chopped straw in water for several hours or overnight to hydrate it.

3. Pasteurization with Calcium Oxide

  1. Prepare Lime Bath:

    • Mix calcium oxide with water to create a high-pH solution. A typical ratio is about 1 cup of calcium oxide per gallon of water.
    • Ensure the solution is well-mixed.

  2. Pasteurize the Substrate:

    • Submerge the soaked straw in the lime solution for 12-18 hours. This process raises the pH of the substrate, helping to kill off unwanted microorganisms while allowing beneficial fungi to thrive.
    • After soaking, drain the straw and rinse it thoroughly with clean water to remove excess lime.

4. Inoculation

  1. Mix Spawn with Substrate:

    • In a clean environment, mix the hydrated and pasteurized straw with oyster mushroom spawn at a rate of about 5-10% spawn by weight.
    • Ensure even distribution of spawn throughout the substrate.

  2. Fill Containers:

    • Pack the inoculated substrate into clean containers or plastic bags. Leave some space at the top for air exchange.

  3. Seal Containers:

    • If using bags, seal them with a breathable filter patch or make small holes for gas exchange.

5. Incubation

  1. Create Ideal Conditions:

    • Place the filled containers in a dark, warm location (20-24°C or 68-75°F) with high humidity (around 80%).
    • Monitor for signs of contamination; healthy mycelium should appear white and fluffy.

  2. Wait for Colonization:

    • Allow 2-3 weeks for the mycelium to fully colonize the substrate. The substrate should turn completely white.

6. Fruiting Conditions

  1. Initiate Fruiting:

    • Once fully colonized, move the containers to a well-lit area with indirect sunlight.
    • Cut slits or holes in the bags or containers to allow mushrooms to grow out.

  2. Maintain Humidity and Fresh Air:

    • Mist the substrate twice a day to maintain humidity levels around 70-80%.
    • Ensure good air circulation to prevent mold growth.

  3. Temperature Control:

    • Keep temperatures within an optimal range (12-18°C or 54-64°F) during fruiting.

7. Harvesting

  1. Timing:

    • Mushrooms are ready to harvest when their caps begin to uncurl and lighten in color (usually within 5-10 days after initiating fruiting).

  2. Harvest Method:

    • Use clean hands or a knife to gently twist or cut mushrooms at their base without damaging surrounding mycelium.

8. Post-Harvest Care

  1. Flushes:

    • After harvesting, soak the substrate in water overnight to rehydrate and encourage additional flushes of mushrooms.
    • Repeat misting and care as before; expect multiple flushes over several weeks.

  2. Composting:

    • After all flushes are complete, consider composting the spent substrate as it can enrich garden soil.
